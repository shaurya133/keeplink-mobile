KeepLink

Play Store Submission Roadmap

Expo SDK 57 · React Native ~2–3 weeks total $25 one-time developer fee
Critical blocker: The app currently hardcodes 10.0.2.2:3000 (emulator localhost). Before any real-device or store testing, app.json must point to your Railway production URL. Everything else depends on this.
Phases
1
Phase 1
Point app at production backend
~1 hour Blocker
  • Update app.json extra.apiUrl from 10.0.2.2:3000 to your Railway URL
  • Verify all Railway env vars are set: RESEND_API_KEY, ANTHROPIC_API_KEY, AUTH_SECRET, DATABASE_URL, EMAIL_FROM
  • Do a full end-to-end test on a physical device against Railway — OTP email, save a link, open AI chat
  • Remove the [DEV] OTP code console.log from lib/mailer.ts before shipping
2
Phase 2
Privacy policy & legal
~2 hours Required by Google
  • Write and host a privacy policy — Google requires a public URL. Minimum: what data you collect (email, saved URLs), how it's stored, deletion contact. Simplest: a plain page on your domain or a Notion page made public.
  • Decide on data retention policy — right now there's no account deletion flow
  • Add a Delete account option in the app — Play Store policy requires it for apps with sign-in
3
Phase 3
EAS Build — signed production APK/AAB
~half day
  • Create an Expo account at expo.dev and install EAS CLI
  • Run eas build:configure — generates eas.json with internal / preview / production profiles
  • EAS can generate and manage your signing keystore automatically — let it; keep a backup of the keystore file (losing it means you can't update the app)
  • Run the production build targeting Android App Bundle (.aab)
$ npm install -g eas-cli
$ eas login
$ eas build:configure
$ eas build --platform android --profile production
4
Phase 4
Store listing assets
1–2 days
  • App icon — 512×512 PNG, no transparency. KeepLink's chain-link mark works well; just export at size.
  • Feature graphic — 1024×500 PNG. The banner shown at top of the Play Store listing. A simple branded lockup is fine.
  • Screenshots — at least 2, up to 8. Phone (1080×1920 or 9:16 ratio). Capture: login screen, links list with saved items, AI chat, share intent in action.
  • Short description — 80 characters max. E.g. "Save links from anywhere. Read later, search, and chat with AI."
  • Full description — up to 4,000 chars. Explain the share-sheet workflow; that's the differentiator.
5
Phase 5
Google Play Console setup
~1 day $25 fee
  • Pay the one-time $25 Google Play developer registration fee at play.google.com/console
  • Create the app entry — package name must match com.keeplink.app exactly and can never be changed
  • Complete the App content questionnaires: content rating, target audience (17+ is simplest), data safety section
  • Data safety form: declare that you collect email address and user-generated content (saved URLs); data is not shared with third parties; encryption in transit is used
  • Link your privacy policy URL
6
Phase 6
Internal testing → production
~1 week
  • Upload the .aab to the Internal testing track — no Google review, instant availability to up to 100 testers via email invite
  • Test on real Android devices: OTP flow, share sheet, AI chat, link management
  • When satisfied, promote to Production — Google review typically takes 1–7 business days for a first submission
  • Start rollout at 20% of users, monitor for crashes, ramp to 100% Play Console shows ANR and crash data — watch this for the first 48 hours.
After launch
  • Updates — run eas build --profile production and upload new .aab to Play Console. Updates go live in hours, not days.
  • Crash monitoring — integrate Sentry (@sentry/react-native) before launch; Play Console's built-in reporting is basic
  • iOS — same Expo codebase, but requires a paid Apple Developer account ($99/yr) and EAS build for iOS. Submit to App Store Review separately.
  • OTA updates — Expo Updates (eas update) lets you push JS-only fixes instantly without a full Play Store submission. Worth setting up for quick patches.
